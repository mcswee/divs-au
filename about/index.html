About the Project

The Archive

This project is a digital archive of Australia’s federal electoral landscape, spanning from the inaugural 1901 election to the present day. It serves as a visual record of how democratic boundaries have shifted, expanded, and been redefined over more than a century of political history.

The goal is to provide a high-fidelity, easy-to-use mapping interface that allows users to navigate every iteration of the Australian federal map.

The Data

The historical data—including electoral boundaries, division namesakes, and seat results—was primarily sourced from Wikipedia’s extensive historical records and cross-referenced with Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) archives. While Wikipedia provided the foundation, the data has been manually cleaned, audited, and restructured to ensure consistency across disparate eras of record-keeping.

The Build

The site is built as a lightweight, client-side application using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It utilizes GeoJSON data to render historical boundaries dynamically.
