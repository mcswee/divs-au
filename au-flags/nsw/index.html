Design and symbolism

The redesign for New South Wales integrates the unofficial state colour of sky blue and the state floral emblem with the Australian pale. By utilising the specific sky blue associated with NSW heritage and the distinctive Waratah, the flag becomes instantly recognisable while adhering to the unified national system.

State sky blue

The field uses the traditional sky blue of New South Wales, representing the state's coastal identity and its long-standing sporting traditions.

The Waratah

Specifically chosen for the New South Wales design, the Waratah serves as a bold central emblem, representing the unique flora and resilient character of the state.

Unique Southern Cross

To distinguish NSW from other states, this version of the Southern Cross uses a specific point-count geometry of 7,8,6,5, and 9 points.

Governor of New South Wales

The vice-regal standard for New South Wales, Australian pale and the state's floral and heraldic colours.
