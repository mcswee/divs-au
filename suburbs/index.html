How to pronounce the names of Melbourne suburbs

There are a couple of suburb pronunciation guides around and sure, they'll list Prahran and Malvern and Reservoir and maybe even Truganina. But then they'll have whacky phonetics and you can tell they were written by someone with no concept of Melbourne or with no understanding of English phonology. Not that I'm claiming to be an expert in linguistics. But here is a full, comprehensive list of every Melbourne suburb, with a consistent and reliable phonology.

Pronunciation key and orthography

This guide uses a custom phonetic system designed to represent the specific nuances of the Melbourne accent. These pronunciations are based on my accent in Melburnian Australian English and include nuances that are often missing in English keys written for British or American English speakers.

To ensure clarity, all vowels use either a unique symbol (like @ for schwa, and æ for a flat 'a') or use two characters so they are always distinct when they appear in that combination together. By contrast, all consonants use only a single symbol or letter. Syllables are separated by dots (·) but stress is not indicated at all.
