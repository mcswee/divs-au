How to pronounce the names of Melbourne suburbs

Most pronunciation guides for Melbourne lazily cover only the most well-known "difficult" places. They’ll list the same ten or twenty suburbs like Prahran, Malvern, or Reservoir—and maybe Truganina if they're feeling adventurous. They hedge their bets on Lalor, suggesting it could be "lay-lor" or it could be "law-lah". Heaven help anyone wanting to know how to pronounce Clematis, Eumemmering, Kealba, or Yuroke.

For the places they do cover, they often mangle the city’s actual sounds through imprecise respelling. Prahran is given as "Prah-ran", "Puh-ran", or just "Praan"; Malvern becomes "Moul-vin"; and Melbourne is generally "Mel-bin". These guides rely on symbols like "aa" or "oul" and simply expect you to know which phoneme they mean, assuming your natural pronunciation will match theirs. They fail to account for the nuances of specific vowels or the critical distinction between a short "i" and a schwa.

This list is a complete record of every single Melbourne suburb (and along with suburbs and towns in parts of the surrounding shires a total of 674 place names) using a consistent phonological framework. It maps the city exactly as it is spoken by locals, using a one-to-one system to capture the real Melburnian accent without the guesswork of wondering if your accent matches mine.

Every pronunciation in the list below includes a phonetic breakdown to explain the symbols used, along with example words. A comprehensive key to pronunciation is also available for a more in-depth look at the symbols and their meanings.
