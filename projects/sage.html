What is SAGE?

SAGE — Surprise Adjusted Game Evaluation — is a power rating model for AFL teams. It tracks underlying team strength across every game since 2010, weighting outcomes by how surprising they were relative to what the model expected.

How it works

Before each game, SAGE calculates a Team Output for each side:

Team Output = Rival Advantage (R11) + Ground Advantage (V11) + Rest Penalty + Last SAGE Rating

R11 — the average winning/losing margin across the last 11 head-to-head games between the two teams at any venue.

V11 — the average winning/losing margin across the last 11 games each team has played at this specific ground.

Rest Penalty — applied when a team has had fewer than 6.95 days since their last game: (days − 7) × 0.2.

Surprise Factor

The surprise factor captures how unexpected an outcome was relative to the model's pre-game read:

SF = 1 − tanh((Opponent Output − Team Output) / 30)

Prediction confidence is derived from (SF_home − SF_away) / 2, scaled to percentage. Both teams always sum to 100%.

Rating change (Δ)

After each game, a team's rating changes by:

Δ = (SF × outcome) + (margin / 60) + Century Bonus + Travel Bonus

Outcome is +1 for a win, −1 for a loss. The Century Bonus (+0.2) rewards teams who score 100 or more points. The Travel Bonus (+0.2) applies when a team has had to travel interstate or to a remote venue (FNQ, NT, overseas).

Starting ratings

All teams were seeded at the start of 2010 using their 2009 final ladder positions, scaled between +1.0 (1st) and −1.0 (last).

Data

All match data is sourced from the Squiggle API. Ratings are recalculated and updated nightly via GitHub Actions.
