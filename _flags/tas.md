description: |
 The redesign for South Australia uses the state’s official colour palette of red, navy, and gold. The navy Australian pale provides a deep anchor for the dark gold Southern Cross, while the red fly creates a striking contrast. The composition integrates the state’s fauna symbol as part of the unified system.
smallprint: |
 This design is similar in appearance to that proposed by many on the internet including <a href="https://bc.id.au/flags/sa.html" class="external-link">Brendan Jones</a>. My version differs in the colour and arrangement of the stars and lack of fimbriation.

  - title: "Piping shrike"
    text: "The badge features the white-backed magpie, or piping shrike, with its wings outstretched, retained from the existing state badge but simplified by the removal of the red and green staff."
  - title: "Southern skies"
    text: "The Southern Cross features stars with 6, 7 and 8 stars.The largest eight-pointed star is positioned at the bottom of the constellation to emphasise the <em>South</em> in South Australia."
