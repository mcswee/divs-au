   <h2>Design and symbolism</h2>
        <p>The redesign for South Australia uses the state’s official colour palette of red, navy, and gold. The navy Australian pale provides a deep anchor for the dark gold Southern Cross, while the red fly creates a striking contrast. The composition integrates the state’s recognised fauna symbol within the unified national system.</p>
        <p class="copyright">This design is similar in appearance to that proposed by many on the internet including <a href="https://bc.id.au/flags/sa.html" class="external-link">Brendan Jones</a>. My version differs in the colour and arrangement of the stars and lack of fimbriation.</p>
      
        <div class="symbolism-grid">
            <div class="symbol-item" style="--state-color:#d50032;">
                <h3>Red, navy, and gold</h3>
                <p>These colours represent the official state identity of South Australia, reflecting the landscape and heritage.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="symbol-item" style="--state-color:#d50032;">
                <h3>The piping shrike</h3>
                <p>The badge features the white-backed magpie (piping shrike) with its wings outstretched, retained from the existing state badge but simplified by the removal of the red and green staff.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="symbol-item" style="--state-color:#d50032;">
                <h3>The southern sky</h3></h3>
                <p>The Southern Cross features stars with 6, 7 and 8 stars.The largest eight-pointed stars are positioned at the bottom of the constellation to emphasise the "South" in South Australia.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
